Foundations of Business

Business involves organizing resources efficiently to provide goods and services, aiming for profit and sustainability. Key objectives include:

  • Profit maximization
  • Customer satisfaction
  • Market leadership
  • Innovation and growth
  • Corporate social responsibility
Question: Explain the conflict between profit maximization and social responsibility.
Answer: Pursuing maximum profit may encourage cutting costs in ways that harm employees, the environment, or society, which conflicts with ethical business responsibilities.

Economics

This section covers micro and macroeconomics principles relevant to business decision-making.

  • Supply and Demand analysis
  • Elasticity of products
  • Market structures: perfect competition, monopoly, oligopoly, monopolistic competition
  • Macroeconomic factors: GDP, inflation, unemployment, interest rates
Question: If a company faces decreasing demand as prices rise, explain using the law of demand.
Answer: According to the law of demand, as price increases, quantity demanded decreases, ceteris paribus. Businesses must price strategically to maintain sales volume.

Marketing

Marketing involves promoting products effectively to targeted customer segments.

  • Market segmentation (geographic, demographic, psychographic, behavioral)
  • Marketing mix: Product, Price, Place, Promotion
  • Branding and product positioning
  • Digital marketing strategies
Question: How does segmentation enhance competitive advantage?
Answer: It allows the firm to target specific consumer groups efficiently, increasing customer satisfaction and loyalty, while optimizing marketing spend.

Finance

Finance management ensures a business can plan, acquire, and manage funds for operations and growth.

  • Sources of finance: internal and external
  • Financial statements: Income statement, Balance sheet, Cash flow
  • Break-even analysis
  • Profit and loss calculations
Profit = Revenue − Costs
Break-even Output = Fixed Costs ÷ Contribution per unit
Question: Fixed costs are $50,000, contribution per unit is $125. What is the break-even quantity?
Answer: Break-even = 50,000 ÷ 125 = 400 units.

Case Studies

Practical scenarios that require critical thinking and applying multiple business concepts.

Case Study: A company’s profits are declining due to increased competition. Propose solutions considering marketing, finance, and operations.
Answer: Solutions include rebranding, adjusting pricing strategies, cost control measures, process optimization, product innovation, and exploring new markets.

Exam Practice

Essay Question: Evaluate the impact of globalization on small domestic firms.
Answer: Globalization expands market opportunities but increases competition. Firms that innovate and adapt can thrive, while those that fail to differentiate may lose market share.
Calculation Question: If sales revenue is $120,000, fixed costs $30,000, variable costs $60,000, calculate net profit.
Answer: Net Profit = 120,000 − (30,000 + 60,000) = $30,000.
